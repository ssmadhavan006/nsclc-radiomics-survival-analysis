# Research Findings and Results

This document summarizes the key scientific and clinical results derived from the execution of the NSCLC Radiomics pipeline on the 421-patient TCIA Lung1 cohort.

## 1. Scientific Findings Overview

### Finding 1: Phenotypic Stage Association
Univariate association testing with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction ($\alpha = 0.05$) revealed that **113 out of 190 cleaned features** were significantly associated with clinical stage.
*   The strongest associations were shape descriptors (e.g. `MeshVolume`, `SurfaceArea`) and wavelet-filtered texture non-uniformity.
*   Advanced stage tumors (Stage IIIa/b) are physically larger, more asymmetric (higher `Elongation`), and texture-heterogeneous, reflecting spatial variation in tissue density and active growth.

### Finding 2: Radiomics Outperforms Clinical Predictors
Our comparative model analysis proved that radiomic features outperform standard clinical staging systems. The Radiomics-Only model achieved a C-index of **0.6396** (95% Bootstrap CI: $[0.6084 - 0.6693]$), which is significantly higher than the Clinical-Only model's C-index of **0.5483** (95% Bootstrap CI: $[0.5182 - 0.5854]$). 

### Finding 3: A 19-Feature Prognostic Signature
Using regularized LASSO-Cox modeling, a 19-feature radiomics signature was constructed. The signature includes shape, first-order intensity, and wavelet texture features. This signature divides the patient cohort into distinct high-risk and low-risk survival groups with high statistical significance (log-rank test, $p < 0.001$).

### Finding 4: Independent Survival Prognostication
In multivariate Cox proportional hazard models, the Prognostic Score was the strongest independent predictor of survival (Hazard Ratio = **2.554**, $p = 3.43 \times 10^{-22}$):
*   Standard clinical stage lost its statistical significance in the presence of the Prognostic Score ($p = 0.623$), proving that the radiomics signature captures tumor aggressiveness more accurately than TNM staging.

## 2. Statistical Associations Summary Table

The univariate statistics for the 190 cleaned features evaluated across clinical endpoints are summarized below:

| Endpoint | Test Used | Significant Features (Raw p < 0.05) | Significant Features (FDR Corrected p < 0.05) | Key Finding |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overall Stage** | Spearman Rank | 125 / 190 | **113 / 190** | Strong correlation with size, shape, and wavelet texture complexity. |
| **Survival Status** | Mann-Whitney U | 24 / 190 | **0 / 190** | Univariate testing lacks statistical power under FDR; multivariate modeling is necessary. |
| **Histology** | Kruskal-Wallis | 18 / 190 | **0 / 190** | Weak univariate differences; texture features do not strongly differentiate cell type. |
